Chosen Technology/Topic

One of the earliest instances of limb replacing prosthetics, was recorded in the journal of a French military doctor in the 1508. The intension was to restore some function of the missing limb while also trying to disguise the fact that the original limb was damaged or missing. The NHI provides describes the mechanism used as “an arm that could be bent with a pulley that mimicked arm muscles”.[1]​

1990 The introduction of CAD/CAM software allows for more streamlined designing and fitting making the production process less time consuming and made fittings more accurate.

2020 the introduction of 3D printing allows for cheaper manufacturing as well as making the process accessible to smaller development teams. This was such a large leap in innovation that it is now found in every area of prosthetics.
